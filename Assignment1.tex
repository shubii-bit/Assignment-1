\flushleft{\textbf{Question 1:}}
If A,B are symmetric matrices of same order,then AB-BA is a\\

(A)\hspace{0.2cm}Skew symmetric matrix\\
(B)\hspace{0.2cm}Symmetric matrix\\
(C)\hspace{0.2cm}Zero matrix\\
(D)\hspace{0.2cm}Identity matrix\\
\textbf{Solution:} \\
\textbf
Given, A and B are symmetric matrices, therefore, we have:
		\begin{equation}
		A'=A\hspace{1cm}and\hspace{1cm} B'= B
		\end{equation}
consider\\

 $=>$ \hspace{2cm}         $(AB-BA)'$  &= $(AB)'- (BA)'$\hspace{1.3cm}[∵$(A-B)'$ = $A'-B'$ ]\\ 
 $=>$ \hspace{2cm}             $(AB-BA)'$ = $B'A'- A'B'$\hspace{1.3cm}[∵$(AB)'$ =$B'A'$ ]\\
  $=>$ \hspace{2cm}            $ (AB-BA)'$ = $BA-A$\\ 
  $=>$ \hspace{2cm}            $(AB-BA)'$ = $-(AB-BA)$\\ 

               
            


        
Thus, $(AB-BA)$ is a skew symmetric matrix.
